Dragon Ball Sparking Zero’s Rage Quitting Problem...

Like most fighting games, Dragon Ball: Sparking! Zero has a bit of a rage quitting problem. Part of that is due to the inherently unbalanced roster of 182 characters where some are naturally stronger than others, meaning you can put yourself at a disadvantage by going off-meta. However, most of the issue comes from people simply not being able to accept a loss. The rage quitting problem is one that affects players of all levels, including Twitch and Kick streamer xQC, who in this case, just happens to be the one rage quitting himself.

Qc isn’t exactly known for playing fighting games, but Dragon Ball: Sparking! Zero has been part of his rotation as of late ever since it released. One of his clips made it to the Sparking! Zero subreddit, where other players watched him go off on people who were using “cheese” to win fights.

Honkai: Star Rail Leak Teases Important New NPC

Call of Duty: Black Ops 6 Drops to 'Mixed' Reviews on Steam

Call of Duty: Black Ops 6 has dropped to "Mixed" user reviews on Steam. While many Black Ops 6 players have used their Steam reviews to complain about technical issues with the game, there is no single reason that fully accounts for the shooter's unimpressive user scores on Valve's storefront.

Call of Duty: Black Ops 6 was released on Friday, October 25. Even before it became available to play, the 2024 shooter found itself at the very top of Steam's Top Sellers chart, where it remains as of October 26. And while Black Ops 6 has hence already proven to be a huge hit on Steam, its early player impressions are not universally positive, as underlined by the fact that the game has now dropped to "Mixed" reviews on the platform. This turn of events cannot be attributed to review-bombing, primarily because the shooter's negative user scores on Steam highlight a fairly wide variety of issues.

Silent Hill's Original Art Director Says He Was Worked Too Much to Get a Girlfriend

Original Silent Hill art director and Pyramid Head creator Masahiro Ito has said he was too busy making Silent Hill games to get a girlfriend.

PC Gamer spotted a handful of X/Twitter posts from Ito who made clear that his time as a game developer wasn't all sunshine and daisies — and not just because he was working on the terrifying survival horror franchise.

For a four-year period between 1999 and 2003, Konami released Silent Hill, Silent Hill 2, and Silent Hill 3, and Ito was heavily involved in their creation.

"All the time I was attending my art university or was developing Silent Hill 1, 2, 3, and a cancelled title, I never had any girlfriends cause I had no time for it," Ito said. "I never thought I was a genius, but I was terribly competitive. The only way to beat geniuses is to give yourself up entirely to what you wanna accomplish."
